CRAIGSLIST SALES POST AUTO CATEGORIZATION
  • Team Size : 5
  • Duration : October 2019 - December 2019
  • Technologies: Python, Scrapy, nltk, Sklearn, Topic Modeling
Description

Craigslist has been cumulating a large amount of unorganized and unstructured data. With the pursuit of enhancing website usability and increasing customer satisfaction, our team proposed an auto-categorization functionality in its ad post service. To deploy the auto-categorization feature, we developed text and image classification models.

The “general” category under the for-sale section contains disorganized ad postings, leading to low click-through rate and user dissatisfaction. Sellers with limited understanding of their merchandise usually label their ads as “general”. Buyers are less likely to search for "general" products. Therefore, ad postings under “general” categories contribute least sales and traffic to the total Craigslist trading and visits

Our text classification model using SVM resulted in 62% accuracy while the image classification model using VGG19 (pre-trained CNN model) had a 90% accuracy

My Responsibilities
  • Scrape product information from target page using Python
  • Applied text classification models and test the performance
  • Frequent code reviews.
